Key Academic Formulas

Essential formulas in Mining Engineering with detailed explanations

Rock Quality Designation (RQD)

\[ RQD = \left( \frac{\sum \text{Length of intact pieces > 10cm}}{\text{Total core length}} \right) \times 100\% \]

The Rock Quality Designation (RQD) is a measure of rock mass quality based on core recovery in drilling operations. It's calculated as the percentage of intact core pieces longer than 10cm in the total length of core run.

  • Used in rock mass classification systems
  • Helps determine support requirements
  • Indicates degree of fracturing

Ventilation Pressure Loss

\[ \Delta P = \frac{f L \rho v^2}{2 D} \]

The Darcy-Weisbach equation calculates pressure loss due to friction in mine ventilation systems, where:

  • ΔP = Pressure loss (Pa)
  • f = Friction factor
  • L = Duct length (m)
  • ρ = Air density (kg/m³)
  • v = Air velocity (m/s)
  • D = Hydraulic diameter (m)

Pillar Strength Estimation

\[ \sigma_p = \sigma_c \left( 0.778 + 0.222 \frac{w}{h} \right) \]

The Obert-Duvall formula estimates the strength of rectangular pillars in underground mines, where:

  • σp = Pillar strength (MPa)
  • σc = Intact rock strength (MPa)
  • w = Pillar width (m)
  • h = Pillar height (m)

This empirical relationship shows that pillar strength increases with increasing width-to-height ratio.
